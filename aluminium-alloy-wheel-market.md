<p><p>The aluminum alloy wheel market consists of three primary types: casting, forging, and others. </p><p>Casting involves pouring molten aluminum into molds to create intricate designs, typically offering a cost-effective option for mass production. Forging, on the other hand, uses high pressure to shape aluminum, resulting in stronger, more durable wheels, ideal for high-performance vehicles. The "other" category includes specialized processes like flow forming and machining, which cater to niche markets or specific performance requirements, expanding the diversity of aluminum alloy wheel offerings.</p></p>
